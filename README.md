# Gilgil Stadium
- Gilgil Stadium area is within Gilgil Municipality.
- Positioned between Nakuru City and Naivasha Town along A104.
- Located at the Juntion between two international trunk Routes.
- The A8 road connecting Mombasa to Malaba, and A4 road, That connects Gilgil to North Horr.


- Gilgil is a National Security Hub, hosting Kenya Army's Kenyatta Barracks, National Youth Service Training College , Anti-stock theft  Unit headquarters, the Kenya Armory and the Administration Police Units.


- Dictates the nature of the town's development,

### Previous Planning Interventions
- It's first development plan was drafted and approved in the year 2002.
- It covered approximmately 12 hectares, and consisted of 27 plots with key land uses.
- There has been a tremendous change in land use from the inital plan.

### Land use patterns
- As at 2021, the centre had 84 plots.
- Recreational land use occupies the largest area, followed by transportation and commercial land use.
- Most land is publicly owned and alloted to residents. 
- The land is being fragmmented as the population grows, there's need to safeguard the stadium and recreational area.
- In Kenya, land markets are governed by the private sector, with mminimal public sector intervention mostly through approximmation of stamp duty.
- In Gilgil, most land is purcchased for commercial developments.
- 0.05 of a hectare, commonly refered to as a 50 by 100 parcel.
- a non-developed plot Is estimated at 1.2 million to 1.5 million Kenyan Shillings excluding existing developments.
  
### Environmental issues
- The stadiumm generally looks neat, it however lacks maintainace.
- There is Poor waste management in the built residential areas.
- An eco-firendly investor has setup a waste collection point.
- There are no sewer facilities, relying on septic tanks and pit latrines.
- Waste water is diverted to the roads.
- All roads within the planning area are earth roads that lacks drainage channels.
- Aesthetic pollution is caused by unplanned structures with sub par building mmaterials within the area.

### Human settlements
- Gilgil Minicipality exhibits a linear humman settlement pattern influenced by road networks.
- Majority of housing is privately owned and rented to tenants.
- All materials are locally sourced.
- Most residents reside in houses with stone walls, some mud and roofed with ironsheets.


### Land tenure
- Most residents hold allotment letters.
- Allotment letters discourage landowners from investing heavily in development or seeking financing.
- Lowest rents range from KSh 4,000 to 10,000.



### Water
- The primary water source for Gilgil is River Trasha in Nyandarua.
- Trasha Dam is located 9 km away.
- Water is rationed and supplied twice weekly, on Tuesdays and Fridays.
